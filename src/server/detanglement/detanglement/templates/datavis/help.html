Help

Entanglement is a Big Data Visualization Tool for experts and newbies alike. It is designed to be intuitive, comprehensive and simple, while being empowering at the same time. The following guidelines are more of a philosophical than a technical introduction and I would advise you to take your time and play around with the program. You might find details in your data you had never imagined to see.

The scope of this tool could hardly be bigger - it aims to visualize every geolocalized data set you have, provided you tell the tool how to read it. The first time using it, you will be seeing an all too familiar Google Map - but you do not have to if you do not like it! An SVG-based alternative that I personally like very much is included as well: Kartograph. With this framework, possibly the most beautiful maps on the web can be created. I hope you will give it a try and come to love it as much as I do - and if not, there is still good old Google Maps for you, right?

Once you have made yourself comfortable with the tool and its look, you might want to look at your data more in-depth. This is possible effortlessly. Depending on whether you use Kartograph or Google Maps, the visualization and the filter might act and feel very different. That is because they are. Kartograph has more of a geographically broad way of displaying trends and significance, while Google Maps aims to localize things very distinctly. Both approaches have their advantages and you might switch between the tools, depending on the job you want to have done. A variety of filters - geographical and time-based - are at your service as well. You mgiht want to check them out if you want to have the most accurate picture of your data.

Most of the filtering and merging is happening in the background. Because automation is not perfect, though, and you might want to cross- check things manually from time to time, options for disabling those features are also given. Beware though, the bigger the data set, the bigger the advantage of having a preselection done by the machine. You do not want to drown in your data, do you?
